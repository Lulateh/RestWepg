Falafel

English

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with various herbs and spices. The mixture is shaped into small, round patties and then deep-fried until they become crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Falafel is often served in pita bread or flatbread and is accompanied by a variety of toppings and sauces, including tahini, tomatoes, cucumbers, and lettuce. It is a flavorful and nutritious vegetarian or vegan option and is enjoyed as street food in many parts of the world.
